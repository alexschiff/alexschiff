I can point out my
hometown on my hand.

I grew up from birth to 15 in the Detroit suburb "Farmington Hills." Our claim to fame is being home to the 2008 Miss America winner. Steve Ballmer (Microsoft CEO) and Bill Joy (Sun Microsystems co-founder) were also from my hometown, but we have signs hailing Kristin Haglund.

When I was 15, I moved to Tampa, FL suburb Palm Harbor. Florida is ranked 49/50 in public education, and whether creationism should be taught in schools was a legitimate public debate (yes, those two facts are related). On the plus-side, you can also point out where you live in Florida on your hand by inverting it. Disclaimer: People will look at you funny.

I was born to be a Michigan Wolverine.

But actually. That's me as a baby. I went to all but 3-4 Michigan home football game from age 5-15 and 18-21, and half my family graduated from Michigan. Maize and Blue runs in my veins, and I started college there in 2009.

Life takes some interesting turns, though, and I left UM after my junior year to build a startup, Fetchnotes. Degree or no degree, I'll always consider myself a Wolverine and my education never truly ends.

I'm an external thinker.

I diagnosed myself with something I found on Wikipedia called "external thinking." It basically means I think through communication rather than introspection. So I like to write, even if no one is listening (usually the case).

From 2009-2010, I was an editorial board member, op-ed columnist, assistant editor, and head of the opinion page for The Michigan Daily. While not hustling at Benzinga, I covered financial and economics news, wrote trading ideas and interviewed top investors, entrepreneurs and journalists from around the globe.

It's hard to find time to write nowadays, but I still try. Recently, my work has been published in Forbes, Fast Company and more. You can catch most of my work on my blog or on Medium.
